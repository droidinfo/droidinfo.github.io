Cross-platform compatibility


Cross-platform compatibility is an important consideration when choosing an Android facetime app. If you want to be able to call people who don't have the same app, you'll need to choose an app that supports cross-platform calling.

  • SIP support: SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) is a standard protocol for VoIP (Voice over IP) calls. If an app supports SIP, it can be used to call people who have SIP accounts, even if they don't have the same app.
  • WebRTC support: WebRTC (Web Real-Time Communication) is a newer protocol that allows for real-time communication over the web. If an app supports WebRTC, it can be used to call people who have WebRTC-compatible browsers, even if they don't have the same app.
  • PSTN support: PSTN (Public Switched Telephone Network) is the traditional phone network. If an app supports PSTN, it can be used to call people who have landlines or mobile phones, even if they don't have the same app.
  • Custom protocols: Some apps use their own custom protocols for cross-platform calling. This can be useful if you want to call people who use a specific app, but it can also limit your ability to call people who use other apps.

By considering the cross-platform compatibility of an app, you can choose an app that meets your needs and allows you to call anyone you want, regardless of what app they use.
