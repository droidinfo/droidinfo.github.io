HD Audio


High-definition audio (HD audio) is a term used to describe audio that has a higher sampling rate and bit depth than standard audio. This results in a more detailed and realistic sound quality.

  • Facet 1: Components of HD audio
    HD audio is typically characterized by a sampling rate of 96 kHz or higher and a bit depth of 24 bits or higher. This is in contrast to standard audio, which typically has a sampling rate of 44.1 kHz and a bit depth of 16 bits.
  • Facet 2: Benefits of HD audio
    HD audio offers a number of benefits over standard audio, including:
    • Increased detail and clarity
    • More accurate sound reproduction
    • A wider soundstage
    • Improved bass response
