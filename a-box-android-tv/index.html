Connectivity


Internet connectivity is crucial for a box Android TV to access streaming services, apps, and games. Box Android TVs offer two primary methods of internet connectivity: Wi-Fi and Ethernet.

  • Wi-Fi:
    Wi-Fi is a wireless technology that allows devices to connect to the internet without the use of cables. Box Android TVs with Wi-Fi capabilities can connect to a wireless router, providing a convenient and flexible way to access the internet.
  • Ethernet:
    Ethernet is a wired technology that uses cables to connect devices to the internet. Box Android TVs with Ethernet ports can be connected to a router or modem using an Ethernet cable. Ethernet connections are generally more stable and reliable than Wi-Fi connections, but they require a physical cable connection.

The choice between Wi-Fi and Ethernet for your box Android TV depends on your specific needs and preferences. If you value convenience and flexibility, Wi-Fi may be the better option. If you prioritize stability and reliability, Ethernet is the recommended choice.
