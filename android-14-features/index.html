Privacy Dashboard


The Privacy Dashboard is a new feature in Android 14 that gives you a central hub for managing your privacy settings. This is important because it makes it easier to see what data apps are collecting and to control how that data is used. In the past, you had to go through each app's individual privacy settings to see what data it was collecting and to change those settings. With the Privacy Dashboard, you can see all of your privacy settings in one place and make changes as needed.

The Privacy Dashboard is divided into three sections:

1. Data access: This section shows you what data apps are collecting and how they are using that data. You can also see how often apps are accessing your data and what permissions they have.

2. Data sharing: This section shows you what data apps are sharing with other apps and services. You can also see who has access to your data and what they are doing with it.

3. Privacy controls: This section gives you control over how your data is collected and used. You can choose to allow or deny apps access to your data, and you can also choose to limit how apps share your data with other apps and services.

The Privacy Dashboard is an important tool for protecting your privacy. It gives you a clear and concise view of what data apps are collecting and how they are using that data. You can also use the Privacy Dashboard to control how your data is collected and used.

App Pairs is easy to use. Simply open two apps that you want to use side-by-side, and then tap the App Pairs icon in the recents menu. The two apps will then be opened in a split-screen view. You can adjust the size of each app by dragging the divider bar between them.

Bluetooth LE Audio


Bluetooth LE Audio is a new Bluetooth standard that offers improved audio quality and longer battery life. It is a key component of Android 14 features, and it has the potential to revolutionize the way we listen to music and other audio content on our smartphones and other devices.

Bluetooth LE Audio uses a new codec called LC3, which is more efficient than the previous SBC codec. This results in improved audio quality, especially at low bit rates. Additionally, Bluetooth LE Audio supports higher bit rates than SBC, which allows for even better audio quality at higher volumes.

In addition to improved audio quality, Bluetooth LE Audio also offers longer battery life. This is because the LC3 codec is more power efficient than the SBC codec. As a result, devices that use Bluetooth LE Audio can play music for longer periods of time on a single charge.

Bluetooth LE Audio is a significant upgrade to the Bluetooth standard. It offers improved audio quality, longer battery life, and lower latency. These benefits make it an ideal choice for listening to music, podcasts, and other audio content on our smartphones and other devices.
