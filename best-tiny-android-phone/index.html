  • Aperture: The aperture of a camera is measured in f-stops. The lower the f-stop number, the wider the aperture and the more light that can enter the camera. A wider aperture will allow you to take better photos in low-light conditions.
  • Sensor size: The sensor size of a camera is measured in inches. The larger the sensor, the more light it can capture. A larger sensor will also produce better image quality, especially in low-light conditions.
  • Features: Some cameras also have additional features, such as optical image stabilization (OIS) and electronic image stabilization (EIS). These features can help to reduce blur and improve image quality, especially when taking photos or videos in low-light conditions.

When choosing the best tiny Android phone, it is important to consider your photography needs and budget.
