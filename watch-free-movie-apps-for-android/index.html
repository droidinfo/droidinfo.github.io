Bitrate: The bitrate of a video refers to the amount of data that is transferred per second. The higher the bitrate, the higher the quality of the video will be. However, a higher bitrate also means that the video will be larger in size and may take longer to buffer.

Codec: The codec of a video refers to the algorithm that is used to compress and decompress the video. Different codecs offer different levels of compression and quality. Some of the most common codecs used for movie apps include H.264, H.265, and VP9.

In general, you will want to choose a movie app that offers high-resolution videos with a high bitrate and a codec that supports high-quality video. However, it is important to note that the video quality you experience will also depend on your internet connection. If you have a slow internet connection, you may not be able to stream high-quality videos without buffering.
