Spam filtering


Spam filtering is an important component of Android voicemail. It helps to protect users from unwanted and potentially harmful spam voicemail messages.

Spam voicemail messages can come from a variety of sources, including telemarketers, scammers, and robocallers. These messages can be a nuisance and can even be dangerous. Spammers may try to trick you into giving up your personal information or downloading malware onto your device.

Android voicemail's spam filtering feature uses a variety of techniques to identify and block spam voicemail messages. These techniques include:

  • Caller ID blocking: Android voicemail can block calls from known spam numbers.
  • Voicemail transcription analysis: Android voicemail can transcribe voicemail messages and analyze the text for signs of spam.
  • User feedback: Android voicemail users can report spam voicemail messages, which helps to improve the spam filtering system.

Android voicemail's spam filtering feature is effective at blocking a large percentage of spam voicemail messages. However, it is not perfect. Some spam voicemail messages may still get through. If you receive a spam voicemail message, you can report it to Google by tapping the "Report spam" button in the voicemail app.

Spam filtering is an important security feature for Android voicemail. It helps to protect users from unwanted and potentially harmful spam voicemail messages.
