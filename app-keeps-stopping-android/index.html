The "app keeps stopping" error is a common problem that can affect any Android device. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including incompatible apps, corrupted data, and insufficient storage space. To fix the error, you can try the following steps:

  • Restart your device.
  • Clear the cache and data for the app that is causing the error.
  • Uninstall and reinstall the app.
  • Check for updates to the app and Android operating system.
  • Factory reset your device.

If you have tried all of the steps above and the error is still occurring, you may need to contact the app developer for further assistance.

Restart your device.


When you restart your device, it clears the cache and closes all running apps. This can help to fix the "app keeps stopping" error by freeing up memory and resources.

  • Clears the cache: The cache is a temporary storage area that stores frequently accessed data. Over time, the cache can become corrupted or full, which can lead to the "app keeps stopping" error. Restarting your device clears the cache and frees up space.
  • Closes all running apps: When you restart your device, all running apps are closed. This can help to fix the "app keeps stopping" error by stopping any apps that are causing problems.

Restarting your device is a simple and effective way to fix the "app keeps stopping" error. It is a good first step to try before trying other troubleshooting steps.

Factory reset your device.


A factory reset is a drastic measure, but it can be effective in fixing the "app keeps stopping" error if all other troubleshooting steps have failed. A factory reset will erase all of the data on your device, including your apps, settings, and files. Therefore, it is important to back up your data before performing a factory reset.

Once you have backed up your data, you can follow these steps to perform a factory reset:

  1. Open the Settings app on your device.
  2. Tap on the System menu.
  3. Tap on the Reset options menu.
  4. Tap on the Erase all data (factory reset) option.
  5. Tap on the Reset phone button.
  6. Enter your device's passcode or pattern.
  7. Tap on the Erase everything button.

Once the factory reset is complete, your device will restart. You will then need to set up your device as if it were new.

Factory resetting your device is a powerful troubleshooting step that can fix a variety of problems, including the "app keeps stopping" error. However, it is important to note that a factory reset will erase all of the data on your device, so it is important to back up your data before performing a factory reset.
