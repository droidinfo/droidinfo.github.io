Water resistance


Water resistance is an important feature to consider when choosing an android smart watch, especially if you plan on wearing it during workouts or outdoor activities. Water resistance is measured in ATM (atmospheres), and the higher the ATM rating, the more water resistant the watch is. For example, a watch with a 5 ATM rating can withstand being submerged in water up to 50 meters deep.

  • Everyday use: A watch with a 3 ATM rating is water resistant enough for everyday use, such as washing your hands or getting caught in the rain.
  • Swimming: A watch with a 5 ATM rating is water resistant enough for swimming and other water sports.
  • Diving: A watch with a 10 ATM rating or higher is water resistant enough for diving.

If you're not sure what ATM rating you need, it's always better to err on the side of caution and choose a watch with a higher rating. This will ensure that your watch is protected from water damage, even if you don't plan on doing any serious swimming or diving.

Battery life


Battery life is an important consideration when choosing any electronic device, and android smart watches are no exception. The battery life of an android smart watch will vary depending on a number of factors, including the size of the display, the number of features being used, and the brightness of the screen. However, most android smart watches have a battery life of 1-2 days, which is typically enough to get you through a full day of use without having to worry about recharging.

  • Display size: The size of the display is one of the biggest factors that will affect the battery life of your android smart watch. A larger display will require more power to operate, so if you're looking for a watch with a long battery life, you'll want to choose one with a smaller display.
  • Features: The number of features that you use on your android smart watch will also affect the battery life. Features such as GPS, heart rate monitoring, and music playback can all drain the battery more quickly. If you're looking for a watch with a long battery life, you'll want to choose one with fewer features.
  • Screen brightness: The brightness of the screen is another factor that can affect the battery life of your android smart watch. A brighter screen will require more power to operate, so if you're looking for a watch with a long battery life, you'll want to choose one with a dimmer screen.

By understanding the factors that affect the battery life of android smart watches, you can choose a watch that will meet your needs. If you're looking for a watch with a long battery life, you'll want to choose one with a smaller display, fewer features, and a dimmer screen.
