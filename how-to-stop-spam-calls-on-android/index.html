Enable call screening


Call screening is a feature that can be enabled on Android devices to help reduce the number of spam calls. When call screening is enabled, your device will automatically screen incoming calls and identify potential spam callers. You can then choose to answer the call, send it to voicemail, or block the number.

Call screening is an effective way to reduce the number of spam calls you receive, and it is easy to enable. To enable call screening on your Android device, follow these steps:

  1. Open the Phone app.
  2. Tap the three dots in the top right corner of the screen.
  3. Tap Settings.
  4. Tap Call Screening.
  5. Toggle the switch to the On position.

Once call screening is enabled, your device will automatically screen incoming calls. You will see a notification on your screen that says "Potential spam call" or "Scam likely." You can then choose to answer the call, send it to voicemail, or block the number.

Call screening is a valuable tool that can help you reduce the number of spam calls you receive. It is easy to enable and use, and it can be a major time-saver.

Register with the Do Not Call Registry


The National Do Not Call Registry is a government-run database of phone numbers that telemarketers are prohibited from calling. Registering your phone number with the Do Not Call Registry is a free and effective way to reduce the number of spam calls you receive.

To register your phone number with the Do Not Call Registry, you can visit the Do Not Call Registry website or call 1-888-382-1222. You will need to provide your phone number, name, and address. Once you are registered, telemarketers will have 31 days to remove your number from their call lists.

Registering with the Do Not Call Registry is not a foolproof way to stop spam calls, but it can significantly reduce the number of calls you receive. It is also important to note that the Do Not Call Registry only applies to telemarketers. Political organizations, charities, and debt collectors are not required to follow the Do Not Call Registry rules.

There are a few different ways to report spam calls to your carrier. If you use the carrier's website, you may be able to submit a report online. If you use a third-party app, the app will typically provide instructions on how to report spam calls.

Reporting spam calls to your carrier is a simple and effective way to help stop spam calls on Android. By reporting spam calls, you can help your carrier identify and block spam callers, which will help to reduce the number of spam calls you receive.

Be careful about what information you share online


Personal information, such as your name, address, and phone number, can be used by spammers to target you with unwanted calls. Be careful about what information you share online, especially on social media. Only share information with people you know and trust. Be wary of clicking on links in emails or text messages from unknown senders. These links could lead to phishing websites that are designed to steal your personal information.

  • Facet 1: Social Media

    When you share personal information on social media, it is important to be aware of who can see that information. Your posts may be visible to everyone, even if you only intended to share them with your friends. Be careful about sharing your phone number, address, or other personal information on social media.

  • Facet 2: Public Wi-Fi

    Public Wi-Fi networks are not always secure. When you connect to a public Wi-Fi network, your personal information may be vulnerable to hackers. Avoid accessing sensitive information, such as your bank account or credit card information, when you are connected to a public Wi-Fi network.

  • Facet 3: Phishing Scams

    Phishing scams are designed to trick you into giving up your personal information. Phishing emails or text messages may look like they are from a legitimate source, such as your bank or a government agency. However, these messages are actually designed to steal your personal information. Be careful about clicking on links in emails or text messages from unknown senders. If you are unsure whether an email or text message is legitimate, do not click on any links. Instead, contact the sender directly.

  • Facet 4: Data Breaches

    Data breaches are becoming increasingly common. When a data breach occurs, the personal information of millions of people may be compromised. If your personal information has been compromised in a data breach, be sure to change your passwords and monitor your credit reports for any unauthorized activity.

By being careful about what information you share online, you can reduce your risk of receiving spam calls. Spammers rely on personal information to target their victims. By protecting your personal information, you can make it more difficult for spammers to reach you.

File a complaint with the FCC


The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) is the government agency responsible for regulating interstate and international communications by radio, television, wire, satellite, and cable. The FCC also investigates and resolves complaints from consumers about violations of communications laws and regulations.

If you have been receiving unwanted spam calls on your Android device, you can file a complaint with the FCC. The FCC will investigate your complaint and take appropriate action, which may include imposing fines on the spam caller. Filing a complaint with the FCC is a free and effective way to help stop spam calls.

To file a complaint with the FCC, you can visit the FCC's website or call 1-888-CALL-FCC (1-888-225-5322). You will need to provide the FCC with the phone number of the spam caller, the date and time of the call, and a brief description of the call.

Filing a complaint with the FCC is an important part of stopping spam calls on Android. The FCC needs your help to identify and stop spam callers. By filing a complaint, you are helping to make a difference.

Educate yourself about spam calls


Educating yourself about spam calls is a crucial step in the fight against this pervasive issue. By understanding the different types of spam calls, the tactics used by spam callers, and the laws and regulations governing spam calls, you can empower yourself to take effective action to stop spam calls on Android devices.

  • Facet 1: Types of Spam Calls

    There are many different types of spam calls, including robocalls, spoofed calls, and phishing calls. Robocalls are automated calls that are often used to deliver pre-recorded messages. Spoofed calls are calls that appear to come from a legitimate source, such as your bank or a government agency, but are actually from a scammer. Phishing calls are calls that attempt to trick you into giving up your personal information, such as your Social Security number or credit card number.

  • Facet 2: Tactics Used by Spam Callers

    Spam callers use a variety of tactics to trick you into answering their calls, including using local phone numbers, offering free prizes, and pretending to be from a legitimate organization. They may also use caller ID spoofing to make it appear that they are calling from a local number or a well-known company.

  • Facet 3: Laws and Regulations Governing Spam Calls

    There are a number of laws and regulations governing spam calls, including the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) and the Do Not Call Registry. The TCPA prohibits telemarketers from calling consumers who have opted out of receiving such calls. The Do Not Call Registry is a government-run database of phone numbers that telemarketers are prohibited from calling.

  • Facet 4: Tips for Avoiding Spam Calls

    There are a number of things you can do to avoid spam calls, including screening your calls, using a call blocking app, and registering your phone number with the Do Not Call Registry. You can also report spam calls to your phone carrier and the FCC.

By educating yourself about spam calls, you can take effective action to stop spam calls on Android devices. By understanding the different types of spam calls, the tactics used by spam callers, and the laws and regulations governing spam calls, you can empower yourself to protect your privacy and your peace of mind.
