Question 1: Is it legal to download YouTube videos to my Android device?

The legality of downloading YouTube videos depends on the intended use. Downloading videos for personal, non-commercial use is generally considered fair use under copyright law. However, downloading videos for commercial purposes or distributing them without the creator's permission is illegal.

Yes, once you have downloaded a YouTube video to your Android device, you can watch it offline without an internet connection. Downloaded videos are typically stored in the device's gallery or dedicated video player for easy access.

Yes, many video downloaders support downloading YouTube videos in high definition. However, HD videos require more storage space and longer download times. Users should select the video quality that best suits their needs and device capabilities.

If you encounter errors during the download process, check your internet connection and ensure that you have sufficient storage space on your device. Additionally, try using a different video downloader or updating the app to its latest version.

No, using downloaded YouTube videos for commercial purposes is a violation of copyright law. Downloaded videos are intended for personal, non-commercial use only. Distributing or selling downloaded videos without the creator's permission is illegal.
