Network requirements


For a seamless Wi-Fi calling experience, understanding the network requirements is essential. Wi-Fi calling relies on a stable and robust Wi-Fi connection to ensure clear and uninterrupted call quality. Here's how network requirements impact Wi-Fi calling on Android devices:

  • Signal strength: A strong Wi-Fi signal is crucial for high-quality Wi-Fi calls. Weak or fluctuating signal strength can lead to dropped calls, poor audio quality, and delays.
  • Network stability: A stable Wi-Fi network ensures that your call is not interrupted by sudden disconnections or fluctuations in bandwidth. Unstable networks can cause frequent call drops and connectivity issues.
  • Bandwidth capacity: Wi-Fi calling requires sufficient bandwidth to transmit voice data effectively. If your Wi-Fi network is congested due to multiple devices or bandwidth-intensive activities, it can impact call quality.
  • Network latency: Low latency is essential for real-time communication. High latency can result in delays and echoes during calls, making conversations difficult.

Therefore, when enabling Wi-Fi calling on your Android device, it's important to assess your Wi-Fi network and ensure that it meets the necessary requirements. A strong and stable Wi-Fi connection will provide the optimal conditions for clear and reliable Wi-Fi calls.
