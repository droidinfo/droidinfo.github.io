New accessibility features


Android 12's new accessibility features represent a significant step forward in making the mobile operating system more inclusive and accessible to users with disabilities. TalkBack and Switch Access are two of the most important new features, and they can make a real difference in the lives of people with visual impairments or mobility impairments.

  • TalkBack: TalkBack is a screen reader that reads aloud the content of the screen, including text, buttons, and other elements. This makes it possible for people with visual impairments to use Android devices independently. TalkBack can be customized to meet the needs of individual users, and it can be used with a variety of input methods, including touch, gestures, and keyboards.
  • Switch Access: Switch Access allows users to control their Android devices using a variety of external switches. This makes it possible for people with mobility impairments to use Android devices independently. Switch Access can be used to control a variety of functions, including navigation, typing, and app selection. Switch Access can be customized to meet the needs of individual users, and it can be used with a variety of input devices, including joysticks, switches, and keyboards.

TalkBack and Switch Access are just two of the many new accessibility features in Android 12. These features represent a significant investment by Google in making Android more accessible to users with disabilities. With these new features, Android 12 is one of the most accessible mobile operating systems available today.
