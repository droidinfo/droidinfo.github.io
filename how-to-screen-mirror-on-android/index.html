  3. Select the Display option.
  4. Select the Cast option.

Security


Screen mirroring is a great way to share your Android screen with others, but it's important to be aware of the security risks involved. When you screen mirror, you are essentially broadcasting your screen to another device, which means that anyone with access to that device could potentially see what you are doing. This could include sensitive information such as your passwords, financial information, or personal photos.

  • Unauthorized access: One of the biggest security risks associated with screen mirroring is the potential for unauthorized access to your device. If you are not careful, someone could connect to your device and gain access to your personal information. This could be done through a variety of methods, such as phishing, malware, or simply by guessing your password.
  • Data interception: Another security risk associated with screen mirroring is the potential for data interception. If you are using a public Wi-Fi network, someone could intercept the data that is being transmitted between your device and the display device. This could include sensitive information such as your passwords, financial information, or personal photos.
  • Malware: Malware is a type of software that can damage your device or steal your personal information. Malware can be spread through a variety of methods, including phishing, malicious websites, or infected apps. If you are not careful, you could download malware onto your device when you are screen mirroring.
  • Phishing: Phishing is a type of online scam that attempts to trick you into giving up your personal information. Phishing attacks can come in a variety of forms, including emails, text messages, and social media posts. If you are not careful, you could click on a phishing link and give up your personal information to a scammer.

It is important to be aware of the security risks associated with screen mirroring and to take steps to protect yourself. Here are a few tips:

  • Only screen mirror to devices that you trust.
  • Use a strong password for your device.
  • Be careful about what information you share while you are screen mirroring.
  • Only download apps from trusted sources.
  • Be aware of the signs of phishing scams.
By following these tips, you can help to protect yourself from the security risks associated with screen mirroring.

  • Disable other devices: If you have other devices connected to your Wi-Fi network, try disabling them to see if that solves the problem. Other devices can sometimes interfere with screen mirroring.
