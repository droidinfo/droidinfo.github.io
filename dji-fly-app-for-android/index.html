Intelligent Flight Modes


Intelligent Flight Modes (IFMs) are a key component of the DJI Fly App for Android, offering a range of automated flight patterns that allow even beginner pilots to capture stunning aerial shots with ease.

IFMs work by using the drone's sensors and GPS to automatically control its flight path and camera settings, allowing pilots to focus on framing their shots and capturing the perfect moment.

There are a variety of IFMs available in the DJI Fly App for Android, each designed for a specific type of shot. Some of the most popular IFMs include:

  • QuickShots: QuickShots are a series of pre-programmed flight paths that can be executed with a single tap. They are perfect for capturing quick and easy aerial shots, such as boomerangs, circles, and rockets.
  • ActiveTrack: ActiveTrack allows the drone to automatically follow a subject, keeping it in the center of the frame. This is ideal for capturing shots of moving subjects, such as people, animals, or vehicles.
  • Point of Interest: Point of Interest allows the drone to orbit around a fixed point, keeping the camera focused on the subject. This is perfect for capturing 360-degree shots of landmarks, buildings, or other objects.
  • Waypoints: Waypoints allow pilots to create custom flight paths for the drone to follow. This is ideal for capturing complex shots that require the drone to fly through a specific series of maneuvers.

IFMs are a powerful tool that can help drone pilots of all levels to capture stunning aerial shots. By using IFMs, pilots can focus on framing their shots and capturing the perfect moment, while the drone takes care of the flying.

Safety Features


The DJI Fly App for Android includes a range of safety features that are designed to protect drone pilots and their aircraft. These features include geofencing and obstacle avoidance, which work together to provide enhanced protection during flight.

Geofencing is a virtual boundary that can be set around a specific area. When a drone enters a geofenced area, it will automatically return to its home point. This is useful for preventing drones from flying into restricted areas, such as airports or military bases.

Obstacle avoidance is a system that uses sensors to detect obstacles in the drone's path. When an obstacle is detected, the drone will automatically avoid it by changing its flight path. This is useful for preventing collisions with trees, buildings, and other objects.

Together, geofencing and obstacle avoidance provide enhanced protection for drone pilots and their aircraft. These features help to prevent accidents and ensure that drones are flown safely and responsibly.
