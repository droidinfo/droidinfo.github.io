Frequently Asked Questions about Do Not Disturb Mode on Android

Do Not Disturb mode is a valuable tool that can help you minimize distractions and focus on what's important. Here are answers to some of the most common questions about Do Not Disturb mode on Android:

Question 1: How do I turn on Do Not Disturb mode?

You can turn on Do Not Disturb mode by swiping down from the top of your screen to open the Quick Settings panel, then tapping the Do Not Disturb icon. You can also turn on Do Not Disturb mode by going to Settings > Sound & vibration > Do Not Disturb.

Question 2: How do I schedule Do Not Disturb mode to turn on and off automatically?

You can schedule Do Not Disturb mode to turn on and off automatically by going to Settings > Sound & vibration > Do Not Disturb > Schedules. Tap the "+" button to create a new schedule, then set the start and end times for Do Not Disturb mode.

Question 3: How do I allow certain notifications to break through Do Not Disturb mode?

You can allow certain notifications to break through Do Not Disturb mode by going to Settings > Sound & vibration > Do Not Disturb > Notifications. Tap the "Breakthrough notifications" option, then select the types of notifications that you want to allow to break through.

Question 4: How do I create exceptions for certain contacts in Do Not Disturb mode?

You can create exceptions for certain contacts in Do Not Disturb mode by going to Settings > Sound & vibration > Do Not Disturb > Calls. Tap the "Exceptions" option, then select the contacts that you want to allow to call you even when Do Not Disturb mode is turned on.

Question 5: How do I turn off Do Not Disturb mode?

You can turn off Do Not Disturb mode by swiping down from the top of your screen to open the Quick Settings panel, then tapping the Do Not Disturb icon again. You can also turn off Do Not Disturb mode by going to Settings > Sound & vibration > Do Not Disturb and turning off the switch.
