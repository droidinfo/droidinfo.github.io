Introduced with Android 12, Material You is a new design language that brings a number of changes to the look and feel of Android. It is designed to be more personal and expressive, allowing users to customize their devices to match their own style. Material You also includes a number of new features and improvements, such as dynamic theming, new widgets, and a revamped notification system.

  • Customization: Material You allows users to customize their devices to match their own style. Users can choose from a variety of color schemes, fonts, and icon shapes. They can also create their own custom widgets and wallpapers.
  • Dynamic theming: Material You introduces dynamic theming to Android. This means that the colors of the system UI will change based on the user's wallpaper. This creates a more cohesive and immersive experience.
  • New widgets: Material You includes a number of new widgets, including a weather widget, a clock widget, and a music widget. These widgets are designed to be more informative and interactive than previous widgets.
  • Revamped notification system: The notification system in Material You has been revamped to be more user-friendly. Notifications are now grouped by app, and they can be snoozed or dismissed with a single tap.

Material You is a major change to the look and feel of Android. It is designed to be more personal and expressive, and it includes a number of new features and improvements. Material You is a significant step forward for Android, and it is likely to be a major influence on the design of future Android devices.

Privacy Dashboard


Introduced in Android 12, the Privacy Dashboard is a new tool that gives users more control over their privacy settings. It provides a comprehensive overview of all the permissions that have been granted to apps, and it allows users to easily revoke permissions that they no longer want to grant.

  • Centralized control: The Privacy Dashboard provides a central location for users to manage all of their privacy settings. This makes it easy to see what permissions have been granted to apps, and to revoke permissions that are no longer needed.

The Privacy Dashboard is a valuable tool for users who are concerned about their privacy. The Privacy Dashboard is one of the many new features that make Android 12 a more privacy-focused operating system.

App Hibernation


App Hibernation is a new feature in Android 12 that can help you save battery life by putting unused apps into a hibernation state. When an app is hibernated, it is essentially frozen and will not be able to use any resources, including battery power. This can be helpful for apps that you don't use very often, but that you still want to keep installed on your device.

  • Reduced battery drain: Hibernating apps can help to reduce battery drain by preventing them from running in the background and using up resources. This can be especially helpful for apps that are known to be battery hogs.
  • Improved performance: Hibernating apps can also help to improve performance by freeing up resources that can be used by other apps. This can make your device feel faster and more responsive.
  • More storage space: Hibernating apps can also help to free up storage space on your device. When an app is hibernated, its data is stored in a compressed format, which takes up less space.

App Hibernation is a useful feature that can help you to save battery life, improve performance, and free up storage space on your device. If you're looking for ways to make your Android 12 device more efficient, then you should definitely consider using App Hibernation.

Improved Performance


Android 12 has been optimized to deliver enhanced performance and efficiency, making it a significant upgrade for users. This improvement is attributed to several key factors, including:

  • Optimized Memory Management
    Android 12 employs a revamped memory management system that allocates resources more efficiently. This optimization reduces the likelihood of apps being suspended or terminated in the background, resulting in smoother multitasking and faster app loading times.
  • Enhanced App Startup Speed
    Android 12 introduces a new API that allows developers to optimize the startup time of their apps. This API provides developers with greater control over the initialization process, enabling them to reduce the time it takes for apps to launch.
  • Reduced Jitter and Lag
    Android 12 incorporates a number of optimizations that reduce system jitter and lag. These optimizations include improvements to the garbage collection process, as well as enhancements to the scheduling of tasks on the CPU. As a result, users experience a more responsive and fluid user interface.
  • Improved Power Efficiency
    Android 12 includes several features that improve power efficiency. These features include a new "Battery Saver" mode that can be customized to optimize battery life based on usage patterns, as well as improvements to the way that apps use power in the background.

Collectively, these optimizations make Android 12 a faster, more efficient, and more responsive operating system. This improved performance not only enhances the user experience but also extends the battery life of devices, making it a compelling upgrade for Android users.
